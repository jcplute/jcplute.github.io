Error-correcting codes from toric varieties

Summer 2024

Mentor: Kelly Jabbusch

As part of the REU program at University of Michigan - Dearborn, I and three other undergraduates (Eliza Hogan, Amelia Gibbs, and Nicholas Toloczko) investigated error correcting codes derived from toric varieties and found the minimum distance for a certain infinite class of codes. We also proved several theorems relating the minimum distance of a toric code to its Minkowski length (a geometric invariant of the polytope the code is associated with).

Presentations: Summer Undergraduate Michigan Mathematics Research Conference 2024, Young Mathematicians Conference 2024

Sandpile group of intersection graphs

Summer 2023

Mentors: Joshua Ducey, Brant Jones

As part of the REU program at James Madison University, I and three other undergraduates (Lauren Engelthaler, Jacob Gathje, and Isabel Pfaff) found the sandpile group of intersection graphs. The specific question we began with was finding the sandpile group of Kneser graphs with 3 subsets. This was essentially diagonalizing an arbitrarily large matrix. After implementing past work by Wilson and Bier, we discovered that the same technique applied to intersection graphs in general, not just this particular case, which was a major shift in scope. Our process then generalizes to find a diagonal form of the adjacency matrix of all intersection graphs as well (an infinite class of graphs).

Presentations: SUMS at JMU 2024, Joint Mathematics Meetings 2024

Preprints: Integer diagonal forms for subset intersection relations ( https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.09227 )
